# What Is Functions Anonymous?

*"God, grant me the serenity..."*

The history of programming is littered with interesting ideas. Some of these ideas caught fire and became wildly popular, some of them died quietly and some like the topic of today's issue have been percolating for decades in relative obscurity. The most pertinent thing to me is that merit seems to have had very little to do with any of these outcomes. 

> "A language that doesn't affect the way you think about programming is not worth knowing." - Alan Perlis

It's a real shame that the average programmer will only ever pick up a few programming languages, and the ones they do are almost guaranteed to be a C derivative dipped in Smalltalk bathwater. Woe is us, for we've created a tower of babel and painted it a horrid beige. 

It should be the responsibility of every programmer to fill their toolbox so that when the time comes, they wont feel the compulsion to glue their wrench to their pliers because they need some vise grips. That's what I suppose Functions Anonymous is for: a space to explore different ways to view computation.

To that end I hope there's something here that you find even a little interesting.
